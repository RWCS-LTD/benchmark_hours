Note on extrapolation: A simple January ÷ 6.5 months projection is not used here. Applying a calendar-proportional multiplier to January would project a full-season total of approximately 3× the contract value, which is arithmetically correct but operationally meaningless as a forecast. Note on the net variance. The net variance of +3.63 hrs is the difference between the chain-audited total and the sum of individual form totals. The chain audit credits 123.68 hrs of inter-form operating time — gaps between consecutive forms for the same unit that the contract counts as continuous operation but that were not captured on any individual form. Against this, 120.05 hrs of duplicate and overlap billing was removed. The two effects nearly cancel: 123.68 − 120.05 = +3.63 hrs net. The contractor is not owed the unclaimed inter-form hours — they did not bill for them. The gross flagged excess (est. $9,004–$12,005) is the appropriate figure for credit discussions.
